I have written many Cam Calculation Programs. This is only one of the “family”.  In the picture, there is an example of the part of the data input for an intake cam of a 4-stroke engine actually built.

CFD applied to a 4 strokes engine

This SW calculates the torque and power curves of a 4 stroke engine starting from the size of the single components of the engine itself.  It also calculates  intake and exhaust cams.

The program uses a characteristic method for the calculation of the intake and of the exhaust. The model in fact, makes a computation of the chemical composition of the gas in the pipes.

Using an algorithm for a nonlinear optimization, and leaving free as many dimensions as you want, the program can look for the best solution for torque or power.

Waves in a pipe

This is a small application that uses mechanical fluid  equations to simulate the behavior of a system composed of a plenum, with a known volume, and a tube, with a constant section, connected to the plenum through a valve. The valve opens at t=0 and  it closes at t=0.008 seconds. The flux of gas, due to the opening of the valve,  creates a pressure wave that expands until it reaches the end of the pipe, where it is reflected.

You can change some input parameters, like the volume and the pressure inside the plenum, the total length of the pipe and the position of the pressure sensors.

PLANT

When a company wants to increase it’s production, it must carefully evaluate the best way to reach this goal.  This is a typical optimization problem that requires finding the answer to the following question:

 

               What is the optimal plant configuration that can guarantee my target with minimal investment?

 

Sometimes, when the manufacturing process has an easy flow, the answer to this question is easy and doesn't require too much investigation.  However, there are many plants with very complicated flows. The complication of the production flow often means that one or more of these circumstances are present:

 

  • Production has to change often and single lots must  be maintained separate from each other. Therefore, the production  line must  be cleaned (by sections) between the two consecutive lots.  Time lost during these operations could be very important if the process is slow or if  changing is frequent.
  • There are buffers which need appropriate dimensioning in terms of capacity, inflow and outflow.
  • One or more machines in the plant are intermittent and the conveyors act as buffers between the single machines.

 

In these situations, without a simulator, it is not easy to predict the behavior of the product on the conveyor.  If the speed and length of the conveyor aren't correct, the plant could have unexpected downtime.

For these and many other situations, using simulation techniques makes it easy to predict, with sufficient precision,  the real efficiency of a plant.

To analyze costumer plants  I have developed a SW which starts from the layout of the plant and is able to simulate the real behavior of the plant itself.  If you are interested in understanding more about this SW you can download a demo copy of the  program.
